Please note

Items listed on this site are Halal at least according to the Hanafi madhab.

As such, the restaurants and butchers listed stock hand-slaughtered (HS) chicken and adhere to generally understood cross-contamination standards. Any exceptions will be noted.

How to use this site:
The site is divided into lists for Restaurants, Butchers, and General Foods.

Each entry is classified according to its status, as below:

Verified - Halal: Both the supplier and the seller have been verified by our team OR The product does not contain any problematic ingredients.

Confirmed - Halal: Either the supplier or seller have confirmed that this business is halal/HS, and we are awaiting confirmation from the other party.

Pending Review: We have received a new entry and are currently looking to confirm the status of this business/product.

Partially Halal: A restaurant provides some halal/HS meat, but there is a risk of cross contamination with non-HS or non-halal meat, they sell haram products, etc OR Some varieties/flavours of a product are halal, but not all.

Potential Issues: A company which previously supplied a halal product may now use impermissible ingredients/non HS meat OR A restaurant/butcher will not confirm their supplier OR A product contains impermissible ingredients.

Further details on our verification process can be found here.

Butchers and restaurants often change suppliers on short notice, especially during periods of low availability. There's no guarantee that we can capture supplier changes but we will try our best to highlight which businesses do this.

The halal scene is a rapidly changing one, and so the information in this guide may at times be out of date or incorrect. Please do your due diligence - we are not responsible for what you eat.

FAQ

What's our deal with wholesalers and abattoirs?
Honesty and integrity. Not all information on dishonesty can be publicised online especially where defamation lawsuits are involved. There's no domestic halal certification standard so anyone can certify something as halal. “If I don't certify it, someone else will” is very common. Certification is a race to the bottom; all parties are involved.

What’s the stand on Meat (beef and lamb)?
Machine-slaughter or stunning is not an issue (so far) for beef and lamb. Our issues revolve around the potential for cross contamination with non-halal meats. Wholesalers mix and match abattoirs, to guarantee that the customer (shop/butcher) gets the full quantity of what they ordered. There are fatawa that permit limited cross contamination, but honestly, Muslims deserve better than beef mixed in pigs blood for the reason of laziness - no export halal meat ever touches pigs.

What’s the stand on Chicken?
Hanafis do accept machine slaughter with conditions. These conditions aren't consistently met in Australia. Read more here. Hand-slaughtered chicken is the only method of removing the majority of doubt currently existent in the abattoirs.
Jury is out on CAS (aka gas stunning); a lot of the industry seems to be heading towards it. The topic has been highly politicised and the viability of CAS stunning is still unknown. This means there's no community leadership on the way forward both in terms of ensuring halal certified chicken abattoirs stick with electric stunning, or finding a good way to accept Controlled Atmospheric Stunning as the industry modernises.

What’s the stand on Turkeys?
We understand Turkeys to be hand-slaughtered as they don't fit in machine lines. Please alert us if there's anything to the contrary.

What’s the stand on Ducks?
Hand-Slughtered. Necks are too thick. Please alert us if there's anything to the contrary.

What’s the stand on Quail?
Used to be Hand-Slaughtered exclusively in NSW until ANIC Halal tendered for a contract and said 'we can certify you even if you move to machine slaughtered'. So now it's a mix.
